Development

Learning Moments

1. Some residents prefer alone time

At first, I thought a majority of my residents would be open and excited to interact and participate in programs. I knew some would rather keep to themselves or need some encouragement to join the group. However, I was no prepared for only getting participation from one to two residents regularly. Over time, I found out that some of my residents just did not know about the programs because they did not walk around the areas where posters were hung, or they did not read their messages. Others had been aware; they were just busy or not motivated at the time to join. As I have continued as an RA, I have become more aware of telltale signs of residents wanting alone time versus needing a little encouragement.

2. Teamwork isn't always dream-work

Just as residents may need a push to get out of their comfort zone, so do other teammates when working as a group. As I have worked on the PR Committee in East Village and we as a staff have worked to organized building wide events. In these events, I felt my strengths in taking on administrative roles as well as encouragement and check ins. The events were always on my mind so I constantly thinking of new ways to improve our reach and the event itself.

3. Housing is doing everything they can to develop Norse Life Community

As we introduced the mobile version of the Norse Life Community on Plunk, I have seen a growth of interest over residential life. Even in this tough time in Housing, with the closing of part of Woodcrest and dealing with the virus, I have always appreciated our staffs’ positivity. With the residents always in mind, I feel Housing does its best to deal with each situation thrown their way. NKU is a community and it is the residents and leaders within it that makes it the proud and caring body of people that is the Norse Life Community.

Areas to Improve

1. Interacting with Residents

I always envied how Becca's Honors residents would engage with her so often and be so excited to spend time with her. This semester I tried being more valunerable by opening my door and having games for my residents to play. From my end, I ended seeing people pass and say hi. Going forward I would like to keep this as well as improve by knocking on residents door weekly to get them to play a game or two.

2. Planning Programs

Last semester I issued a poll online to ask residents what types of programs they would like to see. I would like to do this again as well as plan them ahead so I can promote them longer and in different ways.

3. Encouraging Co-workers

Snaps at the end of meeting is always meaningful but I think engaging one on one with co-workers shows how important someone is to a team. I also like to send letters or small gifts to show my gratitude. Next semester, I want to develop team morale by doing little gifts of thankfulness to my co-workers.
